Sustainability Learning Framework

Building Knowledge for a Sustainable Future

R

LO1: Remembering

Recall fundamental facts and terminology related to sustainability

Example Abilities:

  • Identify renewable and non-renewable resources
  • Recognize key terms like "carbon footprint"
  • Name global initiatives like the SDGs
U

LO2: Understanding

Explain and interpret key sustainability concepts and their implications

Example Abilities:

  • Describe how plastic pollution affects marine ecosystems
  • Explain cause-effect of deforestation and climate change
  • Interpret sustainability symbols or waste categories
A

LO3: Applying

Apply sustainability knowledge to real-life situations

Example Abilities:

  • Choose the most eco-friendly product in a scenario
  • Make decisions that reduce personal carbon footprint
  • Apply 3Rs (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) in environments
An

LO4: Analyzing

Analyze systems, problems, or choices related to sustainability

Example Abilities:

  • Compare sustainable and unsustainable practices
  • Detect inefficiencies in energy or waste management
  • Analyze case studies of city's green policies
E

LO5: Evaluating

Evaluate solutions, strategies, or practices based on sustainability principles

Example Abilities:

  • Assess the effectiveness of a composting program
  • Judge whether a government policy is sustainable
  • Rate the sustainability of products or businesses
C

LO6: Creating

Create and propose innovative sustainability solutions

Example Abilities:

  • Design a school-wide waste reduction campaign
  • Create a personal sustainability action plan
  • Propose eco-initiatives for community or workplace
